# Consumer-Behavior-Analysis

"Does Social Media Usage drive Impulse Buying?": Use the .corr() method to prove if your digital marketing efforts are actually causing immediate sales.
"City Tier Opportunity": Use the groupby on city_tier to show where the company should open its next physical store vs. where it should focus on online ads.
"The Tech-Savvy Segment": Use your customer_segment function to identify if "Premium" customers are also the most "Tech-Savvy," helping the product team decide on new app features.

Positive Correlation (Green): If social_media_usage and impulse_buying have a high positive number (e.g., 0.75), you can tell a recruiter: "I identified that social media engagement is a direct driver of immediate purchase intent, justifying a higher ad-spend on platforms like Instagram."
Negative Correlation (Red): If brand_loyalty and return_frequency are negatively correlated, you could say: "Our most loyal customers are actually the most efficient for the business, as they have the lowest return rates, reducing reverse-logistics costs."
The "Tech-Savvy" Myth: If tech_savvy doesn't correlate with monthly_spend, you might suggest: "The data suggests our high-spend demographic isn't necessarily the most tech-forward, so we should maintain traditional store experiences rather than going 100% digital."
